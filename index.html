More About Me

I was born in Taiwan and moved to Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, for three years when I was a child. My father was pursuing his Ph.D. at Carnegie Mellon University during that time. We moved back to Taiwan when he graduated and I entered the National Experimental High School Bilingual Department - a prestigious school in Taiwan with many students who go on to enter top universities in the United States. People from all over the world came to this school, multicultural was a feature of this school and I really benefited from this. This is where I did my elementary to high school. After high school I went to National Central Univeristy, where I majored in communication engineering, but later found myself more interested in computer science. After graduating from college, I served in the Taiwanese army for a year then decided to pursue a computer science degree in the United States and this is how I came here.
